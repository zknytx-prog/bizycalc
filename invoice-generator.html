What makes an invoice look professional

A clean invoice number, clear itemization, and a visible due date reduce payment delays. Clients pay faster when an invoice is easy to scan — vague line items like "services rendered" invite questions and slow down approval.

Invoice numbering that actually works

Use a sequential or date-based numbering system (e.g., 2026-001) instead of random numbers. This makes it easy to track what's been paid, reference invoices in conversations, and stay organized at tax time.

FAQ

What payment terms should I use? Net 15 or Net 30 are standard for most small businesses. Shorter terms (due on receipt, Net 7) are common for freelancers and new client relationships until trust is established.

Do I need to charge tax? Depends on your state, what you're selling (goods vs. services are often taxed differently), and whether you have nexus in your client's state. When in doubt, consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

Can I reuse this for recurring clients? Yes — just update the invoice number, date, and line items each time. Keeping your business name and formatting consistent builds recognition and trust with repeat clients.
